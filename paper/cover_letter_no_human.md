Please consider our manuscript, "Semantic Health Checks for Reproducible Visual-Feature Pipelines: A Two-Renderer Failure Case Study," for the *Journal of Visual Communication and Image Representation*.

The manuscript demonstrates that byte-level reproducibility is insufficient when an image-analysis component is deterministic but semantically inactive. In our case, an unavailable optional OpenCV module silently yielded a constant saliency map across 16,425 Pillow-rendered images and a 1,250-image pairwise-orthogonal OpenCV renderer. Six downstream fields were complete, finite, and hash stable but constant; they represented 15% of the order formula's absolute weight and 45% of hierarchy's.

We replace the fallback with a deterministic in-repository spectral-residual implementation (Hou & Zhang 2007) and introduce four layered semantic-health gates: weighted-feature responsiveness, retained formula weight, rank-equivalent baseline rejection, and exported-score boundary-mass audit. These gates complement existing metamorphic and property-based testing approaches by targeting the specific failure mode of semantically inactive but numerically complete intermediate representations.

A causal column-replacement analysis under development-only scaling shows that repair raises held-out order correlation by 0.047 but lowers hierarchy by 0.263, thereby invalidating a previously favourable result. Target, feature, score, and baseline development are restricted to G-dev; the selected baselines are then locked for G-test. This discipline overturns the previous overbroad claim that all five composites lose to an elementary baseline: order gains 0.045, whereas the other four lose. None of the five formula names is selective for its nominal rendering-parameter target.

The second renderer reveals a distinct failure that feature-level checks alone cannot catch: frozen G-dev score bounds collapse two exported scores to a constant, rendering their rank association undefined rather than zero. The manuscript reports this domain-shift failure without retuning on the holdout.

The contribution is framed as a reusable software-testing pattern and a fully traced two-renderer failure case study, not as a perceptual scale, natural-image benchmark, or independent external validation. We believe this is relevant to JVCIR's scope in visual-feature computation and benchmark reliability, as the failure mode applies to any pipeline using optional dependencies for visual descriptors.
