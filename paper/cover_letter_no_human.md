Dear Editor-in-Chief,

Please consider our manuscript, “Semantic Health Checks for Reproducible Visual-Feature Benchmarks: A Two-Renderer Failure Analysis,” for the *Journal of Visual Communication and Image Representation*.

The manuscript shows that byte-level reproducibility is insufficient when an image-analysis component is deterministic but semantically inactive. In our case, an unavailable optional OpenCV module silently yielded a constant saliency map across 16,425 Pillow images and a 1,250-image pairwise-orthogonal OpenCV holdout. Six downstream fields were complete, finite, and hash stable but constant; they represented 15% of the order formula's absolute weight and 45% of hierarchy's.

We replace the fallback with a deterministic in-repository spectral-residual implementation and introduce fail-fast semantic gates at feature, baseline, and exported-score layers. A causal column-replacement analysis shows that repair raises held-out order correlation by 0.047 but lowers hierarchy by 0.263, thereby invalidating a previously favourable result. Target, feature, score, and baseline development are restricted to G-dev; the selected baselines are then locked for G-test. This discipline overturns the previous overbroad claim that all five composites lose to an elementary baseline: order gains 0.045, whereas the other four lose. None of the five formula names is selective for its nominal engineering target.

The second renderer reveals a distinct failure that feature-level checks alone cannot catch. All repaired saliency fields remain finite and nonconstant, and raw complexity and intensity sums strongly track their nominal factors, but frozen G-dev score bounds collapse both exported 0--100 scores to constant zero. The manuscript reports this domain-shift failure without retuning on the holdout. It also releases 120 renderer--descriptor--target intervals; five of six pre-specified operational descriptors preserve the expected sign across both internal systems.

The editable manuscript, figures, tables, highlights, protocols, code/configuration archive, claim ledger, manifests, and clean-environment verification evidence are included. Every active empirical claim is supported by released controlled evidence. The corrective analyses are identified as `run_20260720_semantic_health_v5` and `run_20260720_external_protocol_v6` and are not presented as preregistered.

The manuscript includes competing-interest, funding, data/code-availability, ethics, CRediT authorship, and generative-AI-use declarations. No human participants or human-judgement labels are used in the active study. We frame the contribution as a reusable software-testing pattern and a fully traced two-renderer failure analysis, not as a perceptual scale, natural-image benchmark, or independent external validation.
